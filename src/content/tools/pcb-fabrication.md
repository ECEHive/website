The Hive has a suite of LPKF tools that allow students to fabricate printed circuit boards (PCBs) in-house, completely free of charge. After completing a three-step training process, you can use the equipment and the Hive's FR4 copper substrate whenever we're open.

## Equipment

### LPKF ProtoMat S103

A 2.5-dimensional milling plotter that drills and mills (cuts) PCB substrates, plastics, and soft metals. Used for routing traces, drilling holes, and board outline cutting.

### LPKF ProtoLaser U4

A 20µm-diameter UV laser for selectively removing copper film from a wide variety of substrate/film combinations. Used for fine-pitch traces and stencil fabrication.

## Materials Provided

- Double-sided copper on FR4 — 0.5 oz/ft² (18 µm) and 1 oz/ft² (35 µm)
- Polyamide and PET film for stencil cutting

## Additional Capabilities

- **Solder stencils** — cut from polyamide or PET (stainless steel via the laser cutter)
- **Reflow soldering** — reflow oven available for SMT assembly
- **Solder mask and silkscreen** — available as unsupported capabilities

## Vias

Electroplating is not supported. For vias, use 1 mm through-holes and solder in a standard header pin. For plated vias, consider a professional fabrication house.

## Training

Training is required and consists of three in-person sessions. Contact us at **[hive-pcb@ece.gatech.edu](mailto:hive-pcb@ece.gatech.edu)** with questions about PCB fabrication.
